# /vendor-comms

---
description: Write vendor outreach emails, follow-ups, confirmations, and briefing documents for Sneakers Fest 2026. Professional, clear, respectful of Lagos business culture.
---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Identify Communication Type

- **Cold outreach** — First contact with a vendor/brand not yet confirmed
- **Follow-up** — Chasing a vendor who hasn't responded
- **Confirmation** — Confirming a vendor's participation after agreement
- **Logistics brief** — Sending setup details, rules, and day-of info
- **Sponsor outreach** — Approaching brands for sponsorship
- **Thank you/Recap** — Post-event message to vendors

### Step 2 — Gather Context (If Not Provided)

1. **Vendor/brand name**
2. **What they sell or represent**
3. **Previous communication history** (cold, warm, confirmed?)
4. **Specific terms to mention** — stall fee, setup time, location, perks
5. **Deadline to include** (registration deadline, payment deadline, etc.)
6. **Sender name/title** — For sign-off

### Step 3 — Write the Communication

**Email Tone Guide:**
- Professional but not stiff
- Warm opener that references the vendor specifically
- Clear value proposition — why Sneakers Fest is worth their time
- Specific details (not vague promises)
- One clear action required from them
- Deadline stated plainly

**Cold Outreach Structure:**
```
Subject: [Brand Name] x Sneakers Fest 2026 — Vendor Invitation

[Personal opener referencing their brand]

[What Sneakers Fest is — 2 sentences max]

[Why they're a fit — 1-2 sentences]

[What's on offer — stall, exposure, audience]

[What we need from them — one action + deadline]

[Sign-off]
```

**Follow-up Structure:**
```
Subject: Re: [Brand Name] x Sneakers Fest 2026

[Brief, no guilt-tripping]
[Restate the opportunity clearly]
[Softer deadline if applicable]
[Open door for questions]
[Sign-off]
```

**Logistics Brief Structure:**
```
Subject: Sneakers Fest 2026 — Vendor Setup & Day-of Info

[Greeting]
[Event date/time/location]
[Setup time and instructions]
[Stall dimensions/what's provided]
[What they need to bring]
[Rules/guidelines — brief]
[Contact on the day]
[Sign-off]
```

### Step 4 — WhatsApp Version
For each email, generate a shorter WhatsApp version (3-5 sentences) for informal follow-up.

## Output Format

```
[COMMUNICATION TYPE] — [VENDOR NAME]

EMAIL:
Subject: [subject]
[email body]

WHATSAPP VERSION:
[short message]
```
